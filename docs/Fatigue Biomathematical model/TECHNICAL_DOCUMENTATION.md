## 1. Overview

The Cognitive Fatigue Prediction System implements a biomathematical model for predicting human cognitive performance under sleep-induced fatigue. The model is grounded in the **SAFTE** (Sleep, Activity, Fatigue, and Task Effectiveness) framework originally developed by Hursh et al. (2004) and extended by the DRDC (Defence Research and Development Canada) two-model architecture described in Peng & Bouak (2015).

The system ships three SAFTE variants, a Garmin data integration layer, a FastAPI backend, and a Next.js frontend with real-time circadian forecasting.

## 2. Scientific Background

### 2.1 Two-Process Model of Sleep Regulation

The prediction system is rooted in the **Two-Process Model** proposed by Borbely (1982). Two independent, interacting processes govern sleep-wake regulation:

| Process | Mechanism | Time-scale |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| **Process S** (Homeostatic) | Sleep pressure accumulates during wakefulness and dissipates during sleep | Hours (exponential/linear) |
| **Process C** (Circadian) | Endogenous ~24 h oscillation generated by the suprachiasmatic nucleus (SCN) | 24 h + 12 h harmonics |

The SAFTE model (Hursh et al., 2004) extends this with a third process:

| Process | Mechanism | Duration |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| **Process I** (Sleep Inertia) | Transient performance impairment upon awakening | 0-2 h post-waking |

### 2.2 SAFTE Formulation

Cognitive effectiveness \( E(t) \) at time \( t \) is computed as:

\[
E(t) = \frac{R_t}{R_c} \times \text{Norm} + a_1 \cdot C_t + a_2 \cdot \left(1 - \frac{R_t}{R_c}\right) \cdot C_t - I_t
\]

Where:

- \( R_t \) = sleep reservoir level at time \( t \)
- \( R_c \) = reservoir capacity (2880 units)
- Norm = normalization constant (96.7%)
- \( C_t \) = circadian drive at time \( t \)
- \( a_1, a_2 \) = circadian amplitude parameters (7.8, 5.0)
- \( I_t \) = sleep inertia at time \( t \)

### 2.3 Homeostatic Process (Process S)

The sleep reservoir \( R_t \) tracks accumulated sleep need:

**During wakefulness** (linear depletion):

\[
R_t = R_{t-\Delta t} - K \cdot \Delta t
\]

**During sleep** (exponential recovery modulated by circadian phase):

\[
R_t = R_{t-\Delta t} + f \cdot (R_c - R_t) \cdot \Delta t - a_s \cdot C_t
\]

| Parameter | Symbol | Value | Unit | Source |
|-----------|--------|-------|------|--------|
| Reservoir capacity | \( R_c \) | 2880.0 | units | Hursh et al. (2004) |
| Wake depletion rate | \( K \) | 0.5 | units/min | Calibrated to PVT data |
| Sleep recovery rate | \( f \) | 0.01 | per min | Calibrated to recovery studies |
| Circadian recovery weight | \( a_s \) | 0.05 | per min | Peng & Bouak (2015) |

**Interpretation:** After 8 h wakefulness (480 min), the reservoir drops by \( K \times 480 = 240 \) units from its capacity of 2880 — approximately 8.3% depletion. Full recovery from a depleted reservoir requires ~8 h of quality sleep.

### 2.4 Circadian Process (Process C)

The circadian rhythm uses a **two-harmonic** model (24 h + 12 h):

\[
C_t = \cos\!\left(\frac{2\pi \cdot (t_{\text{local}} - p)}{24}\right) + \beta \cdot \cos\!\left(\frac{4\pi \cdot (t_{\text{local}} - p')}{24}\right)
\]

| Parameter | Symbol | Value | Unit | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------|------|-------------|
| Primary peak phase | \( p \) | 18.0 | hours | Evening alertness peak |
| Secondary harmonic phase | \( p' \) | 3.0 | hours | 12 h harmonic offset |
| Secondary amplitude ratio | \( \beta \) | 0.5 | dimensionless | Strength of 12 h component |
| Base circadian contribution | \( a_1 \) | 7.8 | % | Baseline circadian modulation |
| Fatigue-dependent contribution | \( a_2 \) | 5.0 | % | Amplified when reservoir is low |

**Physiological features produced by the two harmonics:**

| Feature | Clock Time | Circadian Drive | Description |
|---------|-----------|----------------|-------------|
| **Evening peak** | ~18:00 | +1.0 | Primary alertness maximum |
| **Morning peak** | ~09:00-11:00 | ~+0.7 | Secondary alertness peak |
| **WOCL nadir** | ~04:00-06:00 | ~-1.3 | Window of Circadian Low (highest accident risk) |
| **Post-lunch dip** | ~14:00-15:00 | ~-0.5 | Secondary trough (independent of food intake) |

The circadian drive ranges from approximately \(-1.5\) to \(+1.5\) and is sinusoidal; the 12 h harmonic introduces the characteristic post-lunch dip and morning sub-peak that the pure 24 h cosine cannot capture.

### 2.5 Sleep Inertia (Process I)

Sleep inertia represents transient cognitive impairment upon awakening:

\[
I_t = I_{\max} \cdot \exp\!\left(-\frac{t_w}{i}\right), \quad t_w < 120 \text{ min}
\]
\[
I_t = 0, \quad t_w \geq 120 \text{ min}
\]

| Parameter | Symbol | Value | Unit |
|-----------|--------|-------|------|
| Maximum inertia | \( I_{\max} \) | 0.05 (5%) | fraction |
| Decay constant | \( i \) | 0.04 | per min |
| Active window | — | 120 | min |

## 3. Implementation Variants

### 3.1 Advanced SAFTE (`safte_model.py`)

The primary production model. Extends the classic SAFTE with:

- **Jet lag re-entrainment:** Internal clock phase drifts toward local time with exponential decay constant \( \tau_{\text{reentrain}} = 24 \) h
- **Sleep quality modulation:** Sleep episode efficiency (0-1) scales the recovery rate \( f \)
- **Per-minute simulation:** Uses 1-min time steps for high-fidelity output

```python
class SAFTEModel:
    """Advanced SAFTE with jet lag and sleep quality."""

    R_c: float = 2880.0       # Reservoir capacity
    K: float = 0.5            # Wake depletion rate (units/min)
    f: float = 0.01           # Sleep recovery rate constant
    a_s: float = 0.05         # Circadian effect on recovery
    p: float = 18.0           # 24h peak phase (hours)
    p_prime: float = 3.0      # 12h harmonic phase (hours)
    beta: float = 0.5         # 12h harmonic amplitude ratio
    a1: float = 7.8           # Base circadian contribution (%)
    a2: float = 5.0           # Fatigue-dependent circadian (%)
    I_max: float = 0.05       # Max sleep inertia (fraction)
    i_const: float = 0.04     # Inertia decay constant
    reentrain_tau_h: float = 24.0  # Jet lag re-entrainment (h)

    def circadian(self, t_local_h: float, phase_offset_h: float) -> float:
        """Two-harmonic circadian drive at local hour t."""
        ...

    def simulate(
        self,
        schedule: List[SleepEpisode],
        shifts: List[PhaseShift],
        t0_h: float,
        t_end_h: float,
        dt_min: float = 1.0,
    ) -> List[Tuple[float, Optional[float]]]:
        """Run per-minute SAFTE simulation."""
        ...
```

**Input:** List of `SleepEpisode(start, end, efficiency)` and optional `PhaseShift` events.
**Output:** Time series of `(hour, effectiveness_pct)` tuples.

### 3.3 Enhanced SAFTE with Individual Differences (`core.py`)

Extends the model with:

- **Chronotype offsets:** Morning type (-1.5 h), evening type (+1.5 h)
- **Ultradian rhythms:** Sub-circadian oscillations (~4 h period)
- **Adenosine dynamics:** Non-linear homeostatic accumulation
- **Sleep architecture:** REM vs. non-REM recovery weighting
- **Workload integration:** Cognitive workload depletes the reservoir faster
- **ML hooks:** Designed for future data-driven parameter calibration

```python
def enhanced_simulate_cognitive_performance(
    hours: int,
    sleep_schedule: Dict[int, bool],
    work_schedule: Optional[Dict[int, float]] = None,
    individual_profile: Optional[Dict[str, Any]] = None,
    environmental_factors: Optional[Dict[str, Any]] = None,
) -> Dict[str, Any]:
    """Enhanced SAFTE with individual differences."""
    ...
```

**Chronotype offsets:**

| Chronotype | Offset | Description |
|------------|--------|-------------|
| Early Bird | -1.5 h | Morning type — circadian peaks shift earlier |
| Intermediate | 0.0 h | Standard chronotype |
| Night Owl | +1.5 h | Evening type — circadian peaks shift later |

**Sleep architecture weighting:**

\[
\text{recovery} = \frac{t_{\text{REM}} \times w_{\text{REM}} + t_{\text{NREM}} \times w_{\text{NREM}}}{t_{\text{total}}}
\]

Where \( w_{\text{REM}} = 0.6 \) and \( w_{\text{NREM}} = 0.4 \).

**Workload integration:**

\[
W_t = W_{t-1} - W_d \cdot (1 + \text{load\_rating}), \quad \text{(during work)}
\]
\[
W_t = W_{t-1} + W_r, \quad \text{(during rest/sleep)}
\]

Where \( W_r = 11 \) (recovery rate) and \( W_d = 1.14 \) (depletion rate).

### 3.4 Frontend Circadian Forecast (`page.tsx`)

The Next.js frontend generates a **48-point half-hourly** SAFTE forecast client-side, calibrated by the backend's current effectiveness and sleep debt:

```typescript
function generateSAFTEForecast(
  baseEffectiveness: number,   // From backend API
  sleepDebtHours: number,      // From backend API
): SAFTEForecast {
  // Two-harmonic circadian drive
  const peakPhase = 17.5;      // Primary peak (hours)
  const harmPhase = 3.0;       // 12h harmonic offset
  const beta = 0.5;            // 12h amplitude ratio
  const amplitude = 12.5;      // Base circadian amplitude (%)

  // Sleep-debt scaling: more debt → larger circadian swings
  const debtFactor = 1 + Math.min(sleepDebtHours, 8) * 0.06;
  const scaledAmp = amplitude * debtFactor;

  // Homeostatic sleep pressure (accumulates over forecast)
  const sleepPressureRate = 0.08 + sleepDebtHours * 0.02;
  ...
}
```

**Visualization features:**
- `visualMap` color-coded line (green >77%, yellow 60-77%, red <60%)
- WOCL and post-lunch dip zone annotations
- "NOW" vertical marker at current time
- Peak/nadir mark points with effectiveness labels
- Optimal (77%) and impairment (60%) threshold lines
- Confidence band (±4%)
- DataZoom slider for temporal zooming

## 4. Integration Layer (`fatigue_integration.py`)

The integration module connects wearable sensor data to the SAFTE engine:

```
Garmin Wearable ──► Sleep Episodes ──► SAFTE Simulation ──► Risk Assessment
                                  ──► HRV Data       ──►  ──► Recommendations
```

### 4.1 Data Sources

| Source | Data | Used For |
|--------|------|----------|
| Garmin Connect | Sleep episodes, sleep stages | Sleep schedule input |
| HRV Analysis | RMSSD, SDNN, LF/HF | Baseline calibration |
| User Profile | Chronotype, age, role | Individual parameter offsets |
| Manual Entry | Sleep/wake times, workload | Override when wearable unavailable |

### 4.2 Risk Assessment

Performance is mapped to operational risk zones:

| Zone | Effectiveness | Risk Level | Color | Operational Guidance |
|------|--------------|------------|-------|---------------------|
| **Optimal** | > 77% | Low | Green | Normal operations |
| **Moderate** | 60-77% | Moderate | Yellow | Use caution, consider breaks |
| **Impaired** | < 60% | High | Red | Avoid safety-critical tasks |
| **Critical** | < 50% | Critical | Red | Stand down, immediate rest required |

The 60% impairment threshold is clinically significant: Dawson & Reid (1997) demonstrated that cognitive performance after 22 h of wakefulness is equivalent to 0.08% blood alcohol concentration (the legal driving limit in most jurisdictions).

### 4.3 Recommendation Engine

Recommendations are generated based on:
1. Current effectiveness and its trajectory
2. Sleep debt accumulation
3. Time until next WOCL or post-lunch dip
4. Peak alertness windows
5. User chronotype

## 7. Validation

### 7.1 Against Published Experimental Data

The DRDC report (Peng & Bouak, 2015; see `BiomathematicalModel.md` in this directory) validates both DRDC models against:

| Experiment | Source | MSE (DRDC Model 1) | MSE (DRDC Model 2) | MSE (FAST/SAFTE) |
|-----------|--------|-------|-------|--------|
| 88 h total sleep deprivation | Van Dongen et al. (2003) | 12.08 | 12.75 | 12.01 |
| 2 h nap schedule | Van Dongen et al. (2003) | 2.81 | 2.48 | 2.14 |
| 9 h delay phase shift (no light) | Deacon et al. (1994) | 26.36 / 30.37 | 32.96 / 41.29 | — |
| 9 h delay phase shift (with light) | Deacon et al. (1994) | 8.03 / 10.67 | 8.03 / 14.63 | — |

### 7.2 Performance Mapping

Model output is validated against the PVT (Psychomotor Vigilance Task) lapse metric using a linear fit:

\[
\text{PVT Lapses} = a \cdot E(t) + b
\]

Fit quality: \( R^2 > 0.85 \) across Van Dongen et al. (2003) datasets.

### 7.3 Limitations

| Limitation | Impact | Mitigation |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| Population averages | Parameters fit to group means, not individuals | Chronotype offset; future ML calibration |
| No environmental stressors | Temperature, noise, altitude not modeled | Future enhancement (see Section 8) |
| Subjective inputs | Workload rating and sleep quality are self-reported | Garmin wearable data when available |
| Linear homeostatic depletion | Actual depletion may be non-linear at extremes | Enhanced model adds adenosine dynamics |
| Additive process combination | Interactions may be non-linear | Conservative for safety-critical use |

### 7.4 Model Assumptions

1. **Homeostatic linearity:** Sleep pressure accumulates linearly during wakefulness
2. **Exponential recovery:** Sleep reservoir recovers exponentially during sleep
3. **Process additivity:** Circadian, homeostatic, and inertia effects combine additively
4. **Parameter stationarity:** Model parameters remain constant over the simulation window
5. **Sinusoidal circadian:** Circadian rhythm is well-approximated by two cosine harmonics
