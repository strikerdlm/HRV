## Top 3 — recommendation with tradeoffs

### 1. IEEE Transactions on Human-Machine Systems (THMS) — **PRIMARY**

- **Fit rationale.** THMS' charter is exactly your manuscript: human-machine systems, cognitive ergonomics, system test and evaluation, human information processing in systems and organizations. The audience is engineering-trained HF — they read code, expect equations, and welcome reference implementations. The editor complaints that killed you at AE ("extensive computer code", "less emphasis on analytical methods") are non-issues here.
- **Tradeoff.** Q2 (not Q1), but JIF 4.4 is essentially the same as Applied Ergonomics' 3.4 — and the *scope match is much better*. IEEE format is two-column, ~10 transaction pages (regular paper). Counter to typical word-cap framing, IEEE pages convert to ~6,500–8,000 words *including figures and references in-line* — you will need to cut the manuscript anyway, so the editor's length critique converts directly to IEEE compliance.
- **Risk.** First-decision typically 3–4 months. Reviewers expect formal methods rigor — your equations and weight-profile derivations need to be defensible, not narrative. The MIT-licensed reference implementation is a positive signal here, not a negative.
- **Cost.** **Zero** if you submit traditional (no OA). Confirmed: "No OA payment is required for traditional submission." Overlength surcharge $175/page kicks in only beyond 10 pp (use 10 as your cut target).
- **Indexing.** SCIE, Scopus, Ei Compendex; JCR-ranked.
- **Submission portal.** ScholarOne Manuscripts via IEEE — `https://mc.manuscriptcentral.com/thms-ieee`

### 2. Safety Science (Elsevier) — **STRONG ALTERNATIVE / Q1**

- **Fit rationale.** Safety Science is a Q1 home for *aviation operator readiness methodology*. A 2025 paper in the journal — "A multi-source physiological data-driven method for evaluating the mental workload of operators in remote control environment" (Vol 187, 2025) — is essentially in OPI's neighbourhood and confirms scope by precedent. The journal explicitly accepts methodology papers, framework formulations, and physiological-modelling approaches. Editorial culture is more accommodating to longer, mathematically explicit manuscripts than HF flagships.
- **Tradeoff.** Q1 (better than THMS for citation-game purposes). But review is slower — Safety Science's mean first-decision is ~3 months and the journal has been moving toward higher rejection at desk under Editor-in-Chief discretion in 2024–2025. The framing pivot is needed: **OPI must lead with safety implications**, not with the framework's elegance. Reviewers will ask "how does this prevent loss-of-control or hull loss?" — your discussion currently says "field validation is future work" and that has to be reframed as a safety-monitoring contribution with explicit safety-case logic.
- **Risk.** Editor may still flag length / code emphasis (the AE critique partly carries over to any Elsevier hybrid). Mitigate by aggressive supplement-relocation and a **safety-explicit RQ** in the introduction.
- **Cost.** **Zero** if subscription submission selected. APC is optional (~USD 4,000+ if chosen).
- **Submission portal.** Editorial Manager — `https://www.editorialmanager.com/SAFETY`

### 3. Accident Analysis & Prevention (Elsevier) — **Q1 BACKUP**

- **Fit rationale.** AAP scope explicitly covers "transportation safety (road, pedestrian, **air, rail**, and water safety)" and operator behaviour. The aviation operator readiness framing is on-mission. The journal publishes a steady stream of HRV / fatigue / vigilance papers in transport contexts.
- **Tradeoff.** JIF 6.2 — highest of the top-three. But AAP leans heavily empirical; they prefer studies *with* operator data over methodology papers. Your "single 128-min recording, three task hypotheses" framing will need to be either (a) clearly labelled as an illustrative methodology contribution with concrete validation roadmap, or (b) deferred until you have the Antarctic C-130 campaign data.
- **Risk.** Higher chance of a methods-only manuscript getting bounced at pre-screen than at THMS or Safety Science. AAP rejected papers go through a Receiving Editor very similar to AE's process — so if framing is not tight, you replay the AE rejection.
- **Cost.** **Zero** subscription path.
- **Submission portal.** Editorial Manager — `https://www.editorialmanager.com/AAP`

## Required revisions before any of these submissions

The AE editor's substantive feedback transfers to all three. Don't re-submit unrevised.

1. **Cut to ≤6,500 words** main text. Move engineering verification, code listings, and worked-example walk-through detail to a single supplement file. Pseudocode + DOI/GitHub URL only in the main text.
2. **Sharpen RQs.** Replace "we present a framework" with 2–3 testable propositions:
   - *RQ1.* Does task-calibrated weighting change readiness classification when summary HRV is held constant?
   - *RQ2.* Are theory-derived per-task weights identifiable on operationally-realistic sample sizes?
   - *RQ3.* (THMS) Does the framework support traceable safety-case logic without sacrificing classification fidelity?
3. **Front-load implications**, retargeted per venue:
   - **THMS:** human-machine system design — framework as substrate for downstream classifiers and audit-friendly gate logic
   - **Safety Science:** operational safety monitoring — explicit safety-case mapping, GO/NO-GO defensibility
   - **AAP:** transportation operator state — fatigue/vigilance contributions to incident prevention
4. **Banish all code blocks** from main text. The MIT-licensed reference implementation is a *citation* with a frozen DOI, not a manuscript appendix.
5. **Independent line-edit pass** — the editor flagged readability explicitly. This isn't optional.

## Cross-cutting observations

- **Diamond-OA Q1 in this domain does not exist.** Every Q1 venue in HF/aviation safety/human-machine systems is either subscription-hybrid (free traditional path) or gold OA (paid). The hybrid traditional route is your only no-cost Q1/Q2 mechanism.
- **IEEE traditional path is genuinely free.** Confirmed at the journal author-info level for THMS and TSMC-S. Don't be confused by the IEEE APC list — those fees apply only if you opt into open access at acceptance.
- **Frontiers / MDPI / PLoS / BMC / JMIR** are all gold OA with mandatory APCs and are excluded from this scout's primary recommendations under your constraint.
- **Elsevier Read & Publish agreements** (the 2025–2028 deals) cover hybrid OA *only* and require corresponding-author affiliation with a participating consortium. Colombia's FAC is not in any current Elsevier R&P consortium I could verify, so do not count on a free OA route via that mechanism — submit traditional.
- **Research4Life Group B (Colombia) waiver.** Diego is in Group B (50% APC discount on participating publishers), not Group A (100% waiver). Useful context if you ever pivot to a gold-OA target, but it is *not* a free path.
- **OPI claim envelope.** Across all three top venues, the "claims are limited to framework definition, software, and illustrative demonstration" hedge from your abstract should stay. Do not let a venue framing inflate the claims past what the evidence supports — that's a different rejection waiting to happen.
